About PowFlex.com

This interface reads from the Jer0me onchain ledger: rate bands (policy bands in bps), policy signals per epoch, analyst terminal sessions and votes, and relay feed slots. Connect your wallet and enter the Jer0me contract address above, then click Refresh to load data. Resolve BPS maps a basis-point value to the active band that contains it. Band history and config snapshot are available in the Config and Band history tabs. Roles show the immutable relay, guardian, treasury, and fallback receiver addresses set at deployment.

Quick start

  1. Click Connect to link your Web3 wallet (e.g. MetaMask).
  2. Paste the deployed Jer0me contract address into the input field.
  3. Click Refresh to fetch bands, signals, sessions, feeds, config, and history.
  4. Use the tabs to switch between Bands, Signals, Sessions, Feeds, Resolve BPS, Config, Band history, and Roles.
  5. In Resolve BPS, enter a value 0–10000 and click Resolve to see which band contains that BPS.
  6. Use Batch resolve (below the tabs) to resolve multiple BPS values at once, comma-separated.

PowFlex.com does not store private keys or send transactions; it only reads from the contract. For relay, guardian, or analyst actions, use the FEDar CLI or interact with the contract directly via your wallet.

Data source

All band, signal, session, and feed data is read on demand from the connected Jer0me contract. No data is cached server-side. Block number and stats are fetched at each Refresh. Ensure the contract address is correct for your network (Ethereum mainnet, Base, etc.) and that your wallet is on the same network.

  • Bands: policy rate bands with lower/upper bps and epoch.
  • Signals: policy signal hashes pushed by the relay per epoch.
  • Sessions: analyst terminal sessions with expiry block.
  • Feeds: relay-updated feed slots (value, timestamp, block).
